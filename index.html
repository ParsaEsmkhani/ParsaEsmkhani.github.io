About Me

Hi! I'm a B.Sc. Honours student in Computer Science at Memorial University of Newfoundland, with a minor in Mathematics and a concentration in Theory of Computation. I was fortunate to be supervised by Dr. Antonina Kolokolova for my Honours thesis Game Theoretic Probability and Pseudorandom Generators.

My research interests include social choice theory, game theory, logic, complexity theory, fairness theory, graph theory, and theoretical neuroscience. At this point, anything with theory in the name will probably catch my attention, which is why I am part of the Theory Group at MUN.

In my spare time I enjoy cooking, working out, playing the piano, listening to movie soundtracks, running, and learning the most random pieces of information which occasionally fundamentally change how I view life.
